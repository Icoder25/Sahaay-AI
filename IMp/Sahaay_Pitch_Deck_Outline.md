## Slide 4: The Sahaay Solution

**Visuals:**
- Flow diagram: Conversation → Routine Model → Proactive Alerts
- 3 example routines extracted from conversation

**Narrative (45 seconds):**
"Sahaay works differently. You don't manage the app—the app manages itself.

Step 1: A 5-minute conversation. 'I take BP medicine at 8 AM every morning. My electricity bill is due on the 5th.' That's it.

Step 2: Sahaay builds a living model of your routine and learns your baseline.

Step 3: It notices anomalies. 'You usually confirm medicine by 9 AM. It's 11 AM and you haven't. Everything okay?' That's not a nagging reminder—that's early warning.

Step 4: For caregivers, they get a simple dashboard: 'Routine stable / drifting / needs attention.' Not overwhelming data. Just the signal they need."

## Appendix: Key Talking Points

### On Competition
"Yes, there are reminder apps. But none of them solve for the elderly person who forgets why they opened the app. None of them use AI to notice anomalies proactively. And none are designed for voice/WhatsApp-first interaction. We're not competing with Todoist—we're creating a new category."

### On Regulation
"We're positioning as a lifestyle assistant, not a medical device. No diagnosis, no treatment claims. We recommend users tell their doctors they're using us. We comply with DPDPA (Indian data privacy law). Not healthcare software—lifestyle software."

### On Profitability
"Freemium model means we reach users with no friction. 60% of users eventually pay ₹299/month ($3.60). Care providers pay ₹999/month. We reach cash flow positive at ~20,000 users. This is not a VC-only business."

### On Expansion
"We start in India because it's our home market and the need is greatest (150M elderly). But elderly care, ADHD support, and caregiver tools are global problems. Southeast Asia, Middle East, Africa have similar demographics and low tech adoption. Sahaay's model scales geographically."

### On Differentiation
"Drift detection is our moat. It requires:
1. Understanding user's baseline (AI + conversation)
2. Continuous learning from behavior
3. Anomaly detection (statistical modeling)
4. Context awareness (health, travel, season)

Competitors can't easily copy this. It's architectural, not just feature-level."

## Notes for Presenters

**Timing:** 
- Slides 1–3: 3 min (establish problem)
- Slides 4–5: 3 min (demo solution)
- Slides 6–7: 2 min (business model, traction)
- Slides 8–9: 2 min (GTM, team)
- Slide 10: 2 min (the ask)
- Total: ~12 minutes + Q&A

**Delivery Tips:**
1. Start with a story (your grandmother, a real user)
2. Pause after the problem slide (let it sink in)
3. Make eye contact during demo
4. Use numbers only when they strengthen the narrative
5. End with vision, not financials

**Handling Objections:**

**"Why not partner with existing healthcare apps?"**
→ "Because they're designed for knowledge workers. They'd require a complete rewrite to serve our users. We're building differently from day one."

**"What if people just ignore the alerts?"**
→ "That's exactly what we measure in cohort analysis. Early data shows 85%+ engagement. But more importantly: if they ignore us, that's data. We learn from it and adjust."

**"Is this HIPAA compliant?"**
→ "We operate in India first (DPDPA compliant). In US/EU expansion, yes, we'll certify for HIPAA. But positioning as lifestyle assistant, not medical device, avoids heavy regulation."
