## Journey Moment: The Crisis Intervention (Drift Detection)

### Scenario: Rajesh Misses Medicine for 2 Days

```
DAY 1 (Wednesday morning)

8:00 AM   Reminder sent: "Time for BP medicine"
          No response for 1 hour

9:00 AM   Sahaay: "You usually confirm by 9 AM. 
          It's 9 AM now. Everything okay?"
          
9:15 AM   Rajesh: "I'm not feeling well today"
          [This data = important signal]

Sahaay:   "Sorry to hear. Did you still take your medicine?"

Rajesh:   "No, I don't feel like eating. Scared to take medicine without food"

Sahaay:   "That makes sense. Medicine with food is better. 
          When you feel up to eating, take it then. 
          Don't force yourself now."
          [Recognizes valid concern, not rigid]

11:00 AM  Logs interaction: 
          "Unwell day - user chose not to take medicine due to health state"
          [Important context for later]

Daughter's dashboard shows:
"Dad didn't take morning medicine - reported not feeling well"
[Simple alert, context provided]


DAY 2 (Thursday morning)

8:00 AM   Reminder sent: "Time for BP medicine"
          No response

9:00 AM   Reminder: "You usually confirm by 9 AM"
          No response

12:00 PM  Still no response
          [2nd day in a row - drift score: 7/10]

Sahaay (to Rajesh):
"You didn't take medicine yesterday or today. 
I'm a bit worried. Are you okay? 
Do you need your daughter to check on you?"

Response options:
- "I'm fine, just didn't feel like it"
- "I'm sick, need help"
- "Give me 5 minutes, I'll take it now"

If no response for 2 hours:

CAREGIVER ALERT (to Anjali):
"🚨 UPDATE: Your father
- Didn't take morning medicine (Wed + Thu)
- Last response was Wed morning ("not feeling well")
- Usually 95% consistent

Recommendation: Call him to check in.
[CALL NOW]"

Anjali calls:
"Dad, it's Anjali. Sahaay says you're not feeling well?"

Rajesh: "Oh, I had a stomach issue. Thought I should skip food/medicine. 
But I'm better now."

Anjali: "Okay, take your medicine now. Let me know if you need anything."

Rajesh: (takes medicine)

Sahaay confirms: ✓ Medicine taken
Later sends: "Thanks for checking on me! 
See you tomorrow morning ☀️"

Daughter's dashboard now shows:
"Resolved - Father checked in, back on routine"
[Green indicator, peace of mind restored]
```

**Why this works:**
- Detects anomaly (2 days of drift)
- Provides context to caregiver (why: health, not laziness)
- Escalates proportionally (not a false alarm)
- Restores routine immediately
- Builds trust (Sahaay caught a real problem)

## Critical Moments for Product Design

### Moment 1: First Missed Reminder (Day 1-3)
**User context:** Rajesh is asleep, forgets to respond

**Product response needed:**
- DON'T spam aggressively (triggers distrust)
- DO check in gently after 1 hour
- Explanation-based ("you usually respond by now")
- Offer help ("Is everything okay?")

**Why:** Sets tone for relationship. Too aggressive = user disables. Too soft = user forgets anyway.

### Moment 2: First Deviation (Week 1-2)
**User context:** Rajesh travels and has different schedule

**Product response needed:**
- Ask about context ("You're traveling?")
- Offer to adjust expectations ("Different schedule today")
- Remember the context for future

**Why:** Shows intelligence (understands context, not rigid). Builds trust.

### Moment 3: Caregiver's First Alert (Week 2-3)
**User context:** Anjali gets first dashboard alert about Dad

**Product response needed:**
- Must be RIGHT (accurate drift detection, not false alarm)
- Must be ACTIONABLE ("Here's what to do")
- Must be TIMELY (not delayed)

**Why:** First alert determines if caregiver trusts the system. False alarm = caregiver ignores future alerts.

### Moment 4: First Health Improvement Visible (Week 4-6)
**User context:** Doctor checks blood sugar, it's better

**Product response needed:**
- Celebrate ("You did that!")
- Connect causation (better adherence = better health)
- Reinforce habit

**Why:** Moves from "useful tool" to "life-changing system."
